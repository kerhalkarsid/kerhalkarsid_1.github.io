About Me

I am a PhD candidate at School for Marine Sciences and Technology at UMass Dartmouth (UMassD). I am a "Distinguished Doctoral Fellowship" awardee from the University (2019-20,2021-2024) and am guided by Prof. Amit Tandon.

My deep love for Mathematics and Physics, and my curiosity for nature inspired me to major in mechanical engineering during my undergraduate at Indian Institute of Technology Bhubaneswar and learn about core concepts like fluid dynamics, thermodynamics, heat transfer etc. Curious about the patterns of cyclones, Indian summer monsoon and the dynamics of ocean and the coupling of atmosphere and oceans, I stumbled upon a course named "Introduction to Ocean Dynamics", followed by "Fundamentals of Weather and Climate". My fascination for such phenomenon coupled with a desire to make meaningful contributions to the society ensured that I pursue my PhD Dissertation studying the role of "Diurnal Warm Layers (DWLs)" in governing the ocean dynamics and air-sea interaction in the Bay of Bengal Physical Oceanography and Climate Sciences.

My current research involves studying the DWLs (and the evolution of the upper ocean) using observations from multiple instruments deployed during the MISO-BoB cruise in July 2019 as well as complement the observations with the use of 1-D ocean models. I also tracked the drifters released in the Bay of Bengal during various field programs to unravel interesting science. We just submitted a conference paper in OCEANS 2022 organized by IEEE-MTS, quantifying the spatial gradients in sea surface temperature (SST), its diurnal cycle and the wind speeds over the mesoscale and submesoscale length scales (i.e. order of 1-100 km) during July 2019, highlighting such variability over small length scales even in case of organized tropical oscillations such as the monsoons.
